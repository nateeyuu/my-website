      Earthquakes happen all the time. In fact, there are up to 9,000 earthquakes around the world every day. Fortunately, most of them are so small we can't notice them. But at 4.53 on the evening of 12 January 2010 Haiti was struck by its biggest eartquake in 200 years. Its happen about 10 kilometers below the earth's surface, not far from the capital city of Port-au-Prince. The earthquake lasted for only thiirty-five secound, but it destroyed many building in Port-au-Prince and kill more than 230,000 people.


      Every country in the world sits on top of a large sheet of rock. Earthquakes are caused by movement of these sheets of rock and are measured on the Richter Scale. Most eartquakes are just tremors which measure 3 or less on the Richter Scale. But each year there are about eighteen earthquakes which measure between 7 and 7.9. These are major eartquakes.


      The earthquake that destroyed Kobe measured 7.2 on the Richter Scale. The earthquake in Haiti measure 7 on the Richter Scale. Geologist knew that two sheets of rock met and formed a sixty-five kilometers fault line near Port-au-Print. After the earthquake, they discoverd that the two sheet of rock had moved nearly 2 metres in opposite direction against each other.


      The amout of damage caused by an earthquake depents on many things. A major earthquake can cause very little damage if it occures far away from town and cities. In Haiti there were many building in small space. Many of building were old or very badly built. They collapsed during the earthquake and 1.5 million people were left without home. It is a frightening remider of how much damage earthquake can cause.
